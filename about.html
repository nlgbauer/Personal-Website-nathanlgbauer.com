Background

From Kansas to San Francisco, by way of Tokyo, Omaha, and a lot of data.

I grew up in Leawood, Kansas, a place that taught me the value of community, service, and showing up for people. I studied finance at Creighton University in Omaha, not entirely sure what I wanted to do, but certain I wanted to lead something that mattered.

My first job out of college was at Gallup, Inc., one of the world's most respected research organizations. I joined as a Business Analyst in an FP&A role, and what I expected to be a numbers job turned into something more formative. Gallup wrote the research on employee and customer engagement, and I learned how the best organizations treat their employees and customers. I also watched from the inside as their core business, phone survey research, was being disrupted by cheaper online alternatives. I spent time building models to help the company understand and adapt to that shift. It was my first real lesson in what happens when technology disrupts a data business, and how the companies that adapt early are the ones that survive.

I went to Tuck School of Business at Dartmouth to apply what I had learned at a larger scale. I interned at Rakuten in Tokyo and San Francisco, working inside one of the world's largest ecommerce companies. What I saw there shaped how I think about platform businesses to this day. Rakuten dominated ecommerce in Japan but struggled internationally as Amazon took control of distribution and faster shipping. Where Amazon owned logistics, Rakuten was a pure marketplace, which meant its international growth depended on other revenue streams, particularly advertising. I worked to help Rakuten's US advertising and intelligence business find cost efficiencies and a path to profitability. It was there I understood how advertising, and specifically first-party data, becomes the strategic lever for established players competing against faster-moving upstarts. That insight led me directly to Walmart Connect.

Walmart Connect

Building Walmart's media platform from the ground up.

I joined Walmart Connect in 2019, after following a Rakuten alum. For the past seven years, I have been building the products that define how Walmart competes in the advertising and retail media landscape.

I was the first product manager on the Walmart DSP, launching it from zero in partnership with The Trade Desk. I built the integrations that enabled advertisers to reach Walmart shoppers on TikTok and measure those campaigns against real purchase data. I enabled Meta's self-serve activation within Walmart Ad Manager. I led the clean room integrations that allow Walmart to collaborate with major external partners in a privacy-safe way. And today, I am building agentic AI workflows that automate what used to take human hours to execute.

The through-line across all of it: data, scale, and the belief that the right product at the right moment can change how an entire industry operates.
